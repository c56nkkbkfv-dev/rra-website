About the Alliance

A community-led organization rooted in memory, dignity, healing, and the future of Rongelap families.

This work is about memory, justice, and the future of a people.

Our mission

We advocate for the rights, health, and future of Rongelap people displaced and harmed by nuclear testing. Our work combines community organizing, education, and global advocacy so the story of Rongelap is heard, understood, and acted on.

From elders who remember life before the tests to youth born in diaspora, we carry forward a commitment: our people deserve justice, safety, and the freedom to decide our own future.

What guides us

Survivor-centered organizing, honest education, cultural preservation, global alliances, and long-term advocacy for health and land.
